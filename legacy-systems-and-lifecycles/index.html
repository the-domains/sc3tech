Lifecycle Management

In computing, a legacy system is an old method, technology, computer system, or application program, "of, relating to, or being a previous or outdated computer system."[1] Often a pejorative term, referencing a system as "legacy" means that it paved the way for the standards that would follow it. This can also imply that the system is out of date or in need of replacement.

When is a system a “legacy” system, and when is it an essential part of operations? The term is described on Wikipedia in the quote: as it says, it’s often used in a pejorative sense. In transport, however, the lifetime of systems is far longer than most IT environments: a train put into service in the late 1990s, with control systems designed earlier that decade, could easily still be in service in the 2020s. Upgrading or retrofitting newer control systems is often simply not possible: older systems cannot necessarily provide the level of activity logging a newer system would feature, and may not have diagnostic ports to provide data anyway. This does not mean the data cannot be made available, but it does mean a combination of embedded systems design, communications/networking and data processing skills can be required to analyse the options.

The main disadvantages of “legacy” systems are that formats may be obscure, and detailed understanding of the original design process of those systems may have been lost: it is common to see compromises during a design process, but these are not always clear when looking back. The advantages, however, are clear: known behaviours, systems and experienced staff - and the probability of greater scope for improvement than in a newer environment, at a lower cost than newer vehicles or systems.

By capturing this data, analysing it and applying the things that are learned, maintenance can move from reactive/responsive to proactive and predictive: it may well be the case that this provides a way to extend the life of a current vehicle or even fleet while systems being upgraded, or when fleet replacement is being planned and budgets sought.
